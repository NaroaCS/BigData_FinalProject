About

This website has been developed by Ayaka Yamashita, Hyun-Chae Chad Loh and Naroa Coretti Sanchez as the final project for the MIT course 11.154/11.454 Big Data, Visualization, and Society.

We were interested in exploring how the Covid-19 pandemic might have further increased social inequalities by hitting more those who were already vulnerable, and we were specially interested on youth because we think that living this situation at a young age can be even harder than it is for adults.

This work explores some of the inequalities that we considered to have a big impact in the daily lives of youth. However, we are aware that there are many relevant factors that we are not taking into consideration and, as a consequence, in order to get a full picture for uses such as policy making, a more detailed study should be conducted with more resources and time.

Data sources and methods

Access to Food
SNAP benefits (% households, US Census 2018) is an indicator of food insecurity. Not eating at school due to Covid-19 put more risk on the population that had problems securing food even before Covid-19.
Access to Playgrounds
Using NYCâ€™s park property data, park maintenance budget data, and population data (2019), we visualized how much city budget was allotted for each open space in NYC. Covid-19 deprived access to school playgrounds from youth, and well-maintained parks became alternatives. (Note: In order only to include quality open space sufficient for supporting youth recreation, we removed not-in-quality lots with just a patch of green.)
Access to the Internet
Covid-19 has changed youth education drastically from in-person to online. To understand how much NYC was prepared for this shift, we analyzed internet access data for under 18 years old (US Census, 2018).
Social Vulnerability Index
We use CDC SVI, which uses 15 U.S. census variables to help local officials identify communities that may need support before, during, or after disasters (CDC/ATSDR 2018). In our case, it will help us to identify youth who have been vulnerable.
